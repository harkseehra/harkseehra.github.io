This question I have been trying to find the right answer because that is what I do: find right answers and elaborate on them. But to find the right answer, you have to experience it. So I have experienced it and am experiencing it, since the change is dynamic, which makes it equally the hardest to do. As with all other cycles we have in our life, like the work and life cycle, food and health cycle, car and bicycle (touché). 😊

The change is the cycle of advancement; it could be negative and positive. So, the change, whether you approve it or not, continues, which makes people change and people change their decisions, their patterns, and their lifestyle to achieve what they want. Yet many times, the change is said to be hard because it is hard to change yourself just because people at the office say you should change that thing about you. And as they say that, they sound like making a change is equivalent to a switch, just on and off, but your heart knows it’s hard to change. 🌟

![unsplash](/images/essays-assets/unsplash%20(35).jpeg)

And change doesn’t come by just taking measures to change. For example, to enhance your drawing, the usage of good quality color pens would not make much of a difference and bring about a change. Therefore, in routine, many people fail to change. 

Yet, they claim they tried their best, which is true to one's own mind, whether they want to comfort themselves or face the harsh reality. The harsh reality is that change is hard. For example, a person with an unhealthy weight is advised by a doctor to lose weight, or face severe health risks. The realization of the importance and timing of change is frightening to many people because change requires sacrifice, particularly the sacrifice of all temporary pleasures. 🌟

The process of change begins in the mind, necessitating a shift from negative to positive thinking. To achieve this, one must alter their old mindset. The idea of change, which should always be positive, typically starts with identifying problems rather than fostering hope. This involves enhancing clarity, focus, and overall thinking. While this mental shift can be daunting for many, it's crucial not to fear change. Often, people are more intimidated by the thought of change than by actual change itself. The necessity of change might stem from the desire for relief, goal achievement, or self-improvement, but it's invariably a challenging endeavor. 

It doesn’t take motivation to change, but it takes consistency: showing up daily for change and sacrificing temporary happiness to achieve the ultimate happiness. We call vices the feeling of guilty pleasure and the habits that release dopamine. By indulging in these bad habits, which provide pleasure for a short period, we have lost so much by not changing as time demands, as dreams demand, and as our body and mind deserve. The sacrifice of these addictive habits makes change a lot easier. 🌟

So, in summary, why change is hard is because many people don’t want to change their bad habits, and they are not willing to change them. Trying once is one thing, but trying constantly to make a change obviously adds up to a positive result. So, try harder. Change is hard, but it is worth it. Maybe it feels hard to hit the gym and wake up early, but if you make that into a habit after months, it is worth it. Change is hard because, in short, it is the mixture of your habits. Positive habits derive positive change; negative habits throw you down into a ditch. 💪
