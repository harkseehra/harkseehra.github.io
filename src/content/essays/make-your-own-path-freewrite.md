> ## What life is truly about making X or Y, Red or green, Yes or no choices and how it cost you in life??

> ## Make your own path, create your own way. 🌟

Likewise, it is said that everyone is different, so is their thinking, their life goals, and life choices. But confused people get more confused as each second passes because confused people are unconfident or, let’s say in a professional manner, they do not have expertise in what they are dealing with in life.

So, first and foremost, what they seek to deal with confusions is advice. Seeking advice from family, friends, and even doctors is not a bad thing. It’s human nature to seek validation of our actions, especially if we are unconfident and new to the ever-changing life happening daily.

From simple advice, like asking a friend if you look okay in an orange coat, to bigger life choices like divorces and settlements, people seek advice. 🌐 Whether in a professional manner or personal, that is how life goes. 💬

But there is a big problem which is unconscious: many life decisions are bound by advice. 🤔

The options to explore more and more advice from more and more people, to gain knowledge of what you are dealing with—whether it’s love, finance, or health—can be overwhelming. 💬

People take their own calculated decisions to accommodate what they are seeking advice for. However, in this whole cycle, something happens. The more you seek advice, the more you become unwilling to live out that advice and actually initiate the action for which the advice was taken. 🚶‍♂️🔄

This occurs because the more advice you take, the more it becomes familiar territory to the mind. The mind starts saying over-confidently, "Oh, I've got this!" 💡

This can even lead to procrastination because we become experts in refined matters. But to actually turn that advice into realistic world actions, things might change, and there is always something new to see and to live. 🌍✨

You may feel over-prepared for a small hazard because people love to give unsolicited advice and, oddly enough, sometimes nudes. 🤷‍♀️📲

Because they are experts of fine matter, they love to throw some banter with extra clues. 🕵️‍♂️ Be extra careful, because life is cautious. Life is dangerous, so always stay alert, they say. 🚨

Moreover, the more advice some people seek, the more we call them confused in the world's speaking language. 🤔 For example, "Oh, this person looks confused," and they really are. They rely on advice to do something, which creates awkwardness. It feels like a loss of subconscious connection with these people. 😕 It feels like they are stuck. 🚧

They are stuck on their mental barriers. In today's world, life is built on seeking advice and validation. 🌐 They have forgotten to think deeply for themselves and often look for some friendly advice. 🤝

However, amidst this friendly advice, it's crucial to remember that no two people are the same. Their perspectives on life are different, their life goals are different, and very few are brave enough to show 100% of who they are and what they truly believe. 🌈

Some people can elaborate well, but most give advice as if they are giving it to themselves. What great examples do they have other than themselves to offer advice? 🤔

Take, for example, a businessman and a hippie. Ask them about their life priorities, and you will get different answers if they are speaking and living their truth. 🌿💼

Yet, in the media, they often preach the same message: "Spread and eat love with bread and jam when you wake up." 🍞💕 But remember, do not believe everything you see in the media. 📺❌

As life advice goes, many people tend to choose tried and tested approaches to live and deal with their journeys in careers, love life, finances, and much more. 🤔 Whether it's to make or break themselves, some people are so knowledgeable and trustworthy that they provide professional advice on social media platforms. 📱

The tried and tested ways to live life often include making a quick buck—like investing in that hot stock. 💸 Or perhaps making girls fall at your feet with a simple trick, which usually involves acting like an arrogant asshole. Yeah, no shit, Sherlock. 🚶‍♂️

To genuinely motivate their audience, these advisers often show some 'Chad' who worked out 8 times a week to achieve an exceptional muscular structure. 💪 The simps who watch and take that advice end up getting chewed up and spit out. 🤷‍♂️

I am not saying all advice is bad, but I think and claim it’s bad to take anyone’s advice and give either unless you know the person who you are giving advice and from person who you are taking knows you.

In the advent of living life more on an advice basis rather than a learn-yourself basis, why not explore yourself? 🌱

Open a website and have a look at how things work. Do some thorough research, explore your interests, and work according to your life goals. 🎯 However, learning for yourself takes time.

It is just a couple of minutes that people would spend extra to browse social media, but they become social just to give and take advice. 💬 Just as everyone is entitled to their opinion, it does not mean that just because it sounds familiar, it is right.

A phase of big misinformation is occurring in human history. People say they are checking facts to see if something is really true, so moralists could come ahead and throw some knowledge on a moral basis. 📜

Let's punish and ban the said person who is a bad influence and gives bad advice to young people. It preaches like they said for Andrew Tate. 🚫👎

There's actually a huge loophole that could be fixed with a simple step. This step enables people not to rely on advice or seek validation from others. 🛠️

It is to own yourself fully—realise your potential and your downfalls. 🌟 Life is about being simple. If you can make simple decisions according to your potential and explore what your mind thinks, you could actually solve many problems. 🤔

If you tend to give a thought to how and why, you enable yourself to deal with similar problems in life on your own. You won't need to rely on a friend for simple advice. 🌱

To some extent, life can make problems complex, just like maths. They start with addition and subtraction, but later they ask you to prove a geometrical theorem with Xs and Ys. ➕➖✖️

Life is like that. You wouldn't want to skip and cheat. 🔄 Be prepared to face challenges head-on and take ownership of your journey. 💪

But why skip and cheat? 🤔 Because, as all things happen in life, it is bound by the so-called human-made mechanism: time.

In the early days, from cavemen to even the 1800s, people relied on sunlight to distinguish between morning, day, and night. 🌞 Life was simple: as morning started, you went outside for a walk, ate something when your stomach demanded, worked, and by the end of daylight, you prepared a meal. You ate as night began and slept. 🌙 Life was simple.

But early capitalists, considering solutions to the rising population, demanded that to keep birthing more children, you had to work more. They created the system of time to bind all people into lifespans so people would know their worth as time passed. ⏳ This was revolutionised by North America after Great Britain departed, and they became their own nation. America insisted on living life bound by time, making that time worth something. 💸 You lived to pay extra for things like property taxes, income taxes, and other solutions that promised to save time. Right? Save some time by using that product, luring people into capitalism. 💰

Then came the creation of "eye candies" to keep people entertained. 🎉 Some people live to party: work and party, work so they could party. On the other hand, some people live super productive lives, which others might call boring. They say, "If you’re not partying, what are you even doing on earth?" 🌍

For all life beings, the punishment of time is set upon the middle class. Middle-class people are not financially broken during recession times; they are more broken by not knowing what they want. These people live by advice and follow generic paths. Hundreds of thousands of students enrol in college, but how many of them make into careers? 🎓

If I provide overall estimates, roughly less than 10%. What about the other 90%? They live life as they say they live life. 😕 Successful people hold successful advice for the unsuccessful: life’s happiness is in small things, granted, but only when you have achieved big things in life. 🌟

I say you do not need advice; you need a path. 🛤️ You need a road-map so you can run on your path and make your own way to your dreams. 🌟

It’s about giving thought to what you truly want in life. After all the money, achievements, and dream cars, what is something you actually want to achieve? 🏆🚗 What is something that you want to leave as a mark on Earth, so people would say, "This person did this for the world?" 🌍

Do you think you could be great at some point, maybe if you gather enough courage to work hard and make something? 💪 And if you are not thinking that, are you thinking of living life like some generic, mediocre person? 🙇‍♂️

Remember, life is no lottery. Things happen after actions. 🔄 If these actions are not dictated by your will, not built and thought by you, but rather initiated by some generic advice, then your life will be more chaotic and complicated. 😣

You'll struggle like the general population with various problems: tough financial conditions, no mental satisfaction, and some even with drug use and abuse. 💸🧠💊

It is time for just being free from Matrix, like Top G says hit the gym, be disciplined and start taking some effort for yourself, similar to what I say make your own plan, have a road map, get rid of all your toys and naughty intentions, because it is time to make your dreams true by initiating real life actions but always after you give it a thought.
