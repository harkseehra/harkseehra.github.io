Every human being is different. We can see the physical appearance, the eyes, the nose, the face, the body, and 'the size'. Similarly, in the thoughtful structure of the mind, everyone’s mind is so different. Every human being responds differently to different things, and each one speaks their own version of language. Their language also depends on their mood. I personally noticed that when people tend to enunciate the words more fluently and loudly, they are most likely to be happy, cozy, comfortable, and worry-free. Conversely, their voice tends to disappear when they are sad. 😊

Their voice drops, then comes the face structure. Then you see bulk in their eyes, and later we get to know the person could be in some super stress. The reason for this stress could be disorders, which are made common these days. We turned "this shouldn’t be normal" into "this is normal," and it tells us we have forgotten to ask the question, like why did it happen in the first place? By asking that question to a broken person, we could find out and focus on solutions. 🤔

At least the very first generation was more open, more loving, and more honest with each other, so they actually cared about each other. Over time, the sheer capitalism of greed and competition caused these qualities to eventually drop, which made us focus on the hardest question to answer in the world: whom to trust and whom not to trust? In a world where parents these days have to teach children not to trust anyone and not be kind to strangers, we have entirely stopped being kind to anyone. This leads to people having trouble forming interpersonal relationships. 🌍💔

This is even evident in families these days. The only family structure of parents and children is prevalent in the West. Now, it is the main reason that when children cannot trust their parents entirely, it leads to anxious adults with trust problems. To enhance that, the idea of commonality is being applied not only by the anxious and depressed but also in the general world, which makes people more judgmental and more worried. 😔

But to help these people with such an idea and to benefit society at large, I propose the very thing again. It is my way of saying that everyone is different in every dimension. It is legitimate that there is no general advice because one person can benefit from a certain diet, but some people feel sick with that. So, you don’t know if you are more suitable for a carnivore or vegetarian diet; the only way to find out is to try it and see. People come from different races, and different races tell us that we are not all descendants of monkeys. 🥦🍖

Some human beings look like monkeys as they are constantly climbing and swinging, but some look like pigs, cows, cats, dogs, squirrels, and wolves. It is no strange thing. Different colors, different sizes, and differences in physical appearance all have their reasons. Some humans look sharp, while some look bulgy, and it is for a reason because each race has its own advantage over others. Some races are good in athletic sports, some are in arts, some are in developments, and some are in science and thinking. This should be respected. With this respect, we could put more trust in each other, especially those living in multicultural countries. 🌍

Then it comes to the language - some cultures were able to spread their language faster than others by historical events of their presence across continents. This is another commonality and could be another mastery to master that language for an anxious mind to appear educated. Do people look for your values, or do people look for your role in their life? The option of third-person POV (point of view) could help instead of thinking about the commonality across people. 🌍📚

Everyone is different, every situation is different, every moment is different, and as God has made it, I believe in God. It is a lovely idea to live and believe everything as different, to neglect the terrors of calculating the possible situations, and to put trust in the almighty God, yourself, and the good people around you to feel comfortable, worry-free, and happier. This way, you can speak loudly, clearly, and more naturally. Because it only makes sense to be different, look different, and have your own voice. 🌟

With more of that, your happiness is unique to your own kind, and your choices could and should be different from the general opinion; true to your own self. You could be like 100 different things in 100 different situations that work for you, but for some people, they are so hard in mold that there is no room for flexibility. You could adjust yourself as per the situation, and it is totally okay for your health to take care of yourself first. But for the sake of commonality and so-called morals, people tense up and make wrong decisions. 🌟

The morals of each person could also be different, same with the core principles. Some people tend to speak up when there is discomfort, while some feel the discomfort and call it minor. Is there tolerance built up more, or are they too afraid to speak? Morals should be built upon the goals, which are again different for different people. Not everyone thinks of any weather as bad, and all pets as cute. While it is okay to have your own different opinions, thinking people think the same is a huge mistake and misunderstanding for one’s own health. 💭

Not everyone thinks the same because everyone is different. Some would agree on one thing, and others would not. So, there is no right and wrong; it’s rather a thought difference. As human beings, we must see ourselves as equals, not ahead or super low. The belief in equality helps to eliminate partial bias. The belief in equality comes from the idea of taking the world as it comes and not making up the situation in your mind before it happens. If you make a negative situation in your mind before it actually occurs in real time, your anxiety-ridden behavior could turn that situation into either elements of sympathy from others or the feeling of distrust, making that situation worse as thought before. 🌍

Your actions shape the situation. Turning happy at a party could make you some great memories. However, turning anxious because of many thoughts before the actual event leads you to appear too cautious at the party. People get cautious when they turn to cautious people because feelings and energies are mutual. Your anxious low energy could make you avoided by people. The world will adjust to your actions. Turning cold with opinions about people leads them to believe you are not open-minded. As the cycle repeats, it is because you have the repetitive anxious feeling that everyone and everything is the same, so your ability to analyze and overthink comes in handy. 😊

Moreover, I see some teens speak the same lingo; to some, it is cool to understand each other in social situations. While others replace the lingo with their original language and face problems with their thought structure, as we lose fluency and articulation of thoughts, it can lead to anxiety. Fluency is important, and it can only happen with practice. 

For general practice, be yourself unless you aim to be a speech therapist and need to gather hard language enunciation data. It is okay to be different. While language is one thing, the same goes for style and appearance. Many people try to wear trendy clothes and shoes regardless of their unique style that they could own and identify with. All that anxiety is due to commonality, leading to a loss of one’s own credibility, personality, and will. 🌟

To eliminate anxiety on all fronts, the first step is always to face it and let it go. Facing it is a big issue, and the notion that everything and everyone is the same in their mind is the most fundamental problem. Once they drop these complexes, their eyes can see better and more clearly.

> ***“Be yourself; the world will adjust to give you yourself. Be like other people, and the world will treat you like how we treat machines—generic, instructional, predictable, and boring.”***

>  ***“Make yourself like yourself, not like Elon Musk or Bill Gates. Being your original self is easier than being somebody else.”*** 

> ***“You are rare; rare things sell at crazy prices, or some don’t even sell. Your value is only high if you are rare, not the generic one.” 🌟***
