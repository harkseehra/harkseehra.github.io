Learning is a hard idea, perhaps a hard choice, when you have too many things going on in your mind. It does not stop, and it could not stop because as soon as it stops thinking, the thinking pattern gets disturbed. For that flow disruption, like a body and mind being a car on the road, you continuously need service, maintenance, and fuel, as well as rest. You cannot keep your car running and driving continuously because it could get heated up, eventually break down, and finally, get blasted and rest for good. This metaphor is similar to the human mind, which I see as being too active due to stimulus forces in the world, causing it to forget to stop. 

There is no actual time for winding down or for giving your mind, the engine of your body, time to cool down and rest. Ultimately, this leads to the advent of ‘get it done quickly’ solutions, much like servicing a car ahead of its actual service time to save time. A mechanic might use some quick hack to repair the vehicle; similarly, people resort to drugs in various forms such as substances, tea, coffee, people, music, and sports. While drugs are seen as medically positive and socially negative, in practicality, individuals with common sense understand that if a doctor prescribes one dose but people take it less or more, it is their problem, not the doctor’s. 🚗💡

Some form of relief is sought from the problem of the mind being on a treadmill. This shows people not being in reality; even their eyes are open, their words are spoken, but they are somewhere else in their mind. Because that chosen drug, a relief, influences their perception, so again, it’s your choice to make a decision to what degree you choose to pick your pet peeve, your poison, to stop your mind from actually achieving what you want and actually learning what you deeply want.

The mind is a visual and hearing learning machine influenced by the external environment, and that external environment is the result of your chosen actions. This means even when we don’t want to learn anything, store anything, it just sees the reality broadcasted through the eyes and hears from the ears and learns it. It is also part of the problem because eventually changing your mind for the better is hard. 🤯

But in my perception, as it comes with always exploring, the viable solution is to lessen your dependency on external factors or drugs in which you choose to indulge yourself. Talk your mind and walk your walk peacefully to get through scratches. Instead of finding people, drugs (in the context of substances), eating disorders, and all the world’s chosen gateways, think of how you would deal with it alone.

 Maybe start accepting that you need maintenance and wind down to stop the mind. Meditation and yoga are viable options; exercising with music is another option. But in my opinion, deciding what to learn—consciously or subconsciously—and replacing your time doing that exact thing you want to achieve is crucial. For me, that includes writing, philosophizing, exploring options, and self-improvement in general. 🌟

Moreover, as I have learned, in every drug, reducing activities to be closer to your objectives is very hard and demands sacrifices of your urges to ‘live a life’ for the greater good, which is exactly God’s pathway to life as it has been described. Believe while also closely inspecting if your urges provide you any benefit in the greater run. Then also comes the question of moral conduct: is it not motivated by greed, lust, and instant gratification, which, in simple language, punks call ‘kick’ and ‘boost’? 🤔

Everything in life is dependent on your objectives, motivation, and perception because with that, you manifest the world. For example, taking the decision of being selfish and ignoring the doctrine of high-value quality—equality results in people being the same to you. People can sense it. Changing your routine from a learned one, seeking comfort instead of earning it, is hard. It's hard to change positions, to stop learning and start learning again.

When you stop learning, your urges for new levels of instant gratification with no practical outcomes or benefits take over. To make a dream happen, that cloud of thoughts must be cleared, and the mind must be stopped to see the clean blue sky and relax. To actually think sensibly and practically, it also changes thought structure and eliminates the thoughts you don't need. The notion that you must think about everything is false. Instead, I say, think about what is worthwhile and rest for the rest. There is clearly no obligation to think about, process, and speak about something if it is useless, leads to no real value conclusions, is not entertaining, and is bland. 🌟

Instead as any sort of drugs, a free pass to reduction to no dependency at all, a path must be taken to eliminate the reliance and to do what you want and be what you want to be naturally.
