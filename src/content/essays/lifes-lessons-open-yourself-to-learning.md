The life is a journey that is colorful, joyful, and beautiful if you are willing to see it. Like any beautiful thing, it is not perfect, because beauty is not in perfection but more in crafting your imperfections to blend with your beauty. No person, no thing, no moment, no time, no weather, no season is perfect, yet there is beauty, wisdom, insight, and a learning ingrained into it that makes everything wonderful, but again, only if you’re willing to see it, feel it, and remember it. 🌟

The moment you take a deep breath, exhale, and drop your thinking, you will find the thinking of that time, moment, weather, and what that thing is trying to teach you, giving you a lesson. There is nothing to learn if the world was perfect, so God, as I believe in it, makes it imperfect, rather sometimes very difficult to survive. But if you put your thinking and complaints over what the actual reality is, all you will have gotten are the complaints in your mind, and complaints come with feelings like anger and sadness. At the end of the day, who does it affect? You. 🌟

Rather, understanding the actual situation takes a sacrifice—a sacrifice of you and your thinking for a moment to learn about it. Every day is new, every moment is new, and the weather we see every time of year is new. We tend to estimate it more or less as it happens, but as the world order goes, everything happens for a reason. If there's pollution, then we need to understand the ways of eliminating that pollution, not working our ways with it and reducing it for our own benefit. Because in the end, who does pollution affect? Us, birds, animals, and living organisms. 🌍

Same way, the depth of understanding what the lesson a day in your life is trying to teach you is there for a reason. It’s the reason I have felt and believe there’s a bad time, and there’s a bad time for a reason. The reason for it is to show a lack of achievements, knowledge, and sometimes just the regular old sad feeling, which motivates me to think more deeply, learn, and encode my perception. It would be a lie if I said that is all me, but the lessons I have learned, especially from the weather, say it’s equally important for you to feel the winter's cold and the rain’s wetness as it is for you to enjoy a blooming spring and summer days. 🌦️🌸

So why not celebrate both? If God didn’t make any differentiation with us all—living organisms like trees and plants, animals and humans—while creating atmosphere and giving us a habitable planet to live on, why do we have exclusive rights or problems with it? Just because we happen to have been gifted the ability to think above others and be human beings, I think this privilege has been used in negative ways. As everything happens for a reason and everything is real-time karma, it happens to sting you back. Because of the unlearnt lessons in the midst of negativity and the superiority of yourself and your thinking, it is affecting people in the long run. 🌍✨

It was a wonder why only children and older people have the ability to distinguish this and encode these lessons given by nature and God, and to be able to just be okay with whatever is happening. Even if death comes, it must be okay. People happen to be scared of death, but the real fear with death is not physical; it’s the psychology of, "If I go, then who? Then who will manage it, eat it, and enjoy it?" 🌿

---

The people don’t want to give up their greed, their ego, and enjoying time with themselves and their little world. So I see many people being sad, angered, depressed, and anxious. About 98% of the time, it has nothing to do with the external environment; it’s not about time, environment, or anything external, but about themselves. They have made judgments in their brains to favor themselves, which has made their thinking process so self-centered that they see themselves in problems and problems. 🌍

The simple psychological solution is when you drop yourself out of the problem, then what remains? The problem. The problem to sort out, because now you see yourself as just anyone with problems, and many people come with their problems being unique. In simple words, they think they are unique. That thing tells how much it’s about themselves, that they are pushed back by only one thing: the shadow of themselves, which wants to remain safe behind the person, so it wants the person to stand still at all times. 🌟

So again, my perception is: if you drop ‘you’ from your perspective and be okay as just a leaf on a big tree, the cold reality is only your parents will remember you for a long time if you happen to drop out of this life. So, it’s okay to fall sometimes; what is necessary is that while falling, you understand the journey of yourself and you’re able to regrow and restart. 

But it only happens if you learn the lessons: a moment, a person, and the environment—whether you feel hot or cold, whether you feel happy or sad—it's for a reason, a lesson to be encoded. If we take that lesson and learn new lessons every day to improve our lives, it involves the sacrifice of thinking ‘you’ for a few moments, a few hours, and, if possible, the whole day. Life will be much better. 🍃
