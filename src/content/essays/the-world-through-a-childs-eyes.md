> ## Exploration

Things progress naturally, not by putting thoughts in the way. As the first thought comes to mind, the mind should have trusted that thought. But the challenge is to have the realest, truest thoughts of oneself, not by different personalities in your head saying otherwise **(ADHD)**. One thought hurts another personality, leading to chaos. Now, we are uncertain, anxious, not in control.

When one part of the personality makes a decision, it may be agony, anger, or anxiety talking. This leads to thinking about how that part of the personality was born. You think it’s wrong and makes bad decisions for minor inconveniences. 

Instead of solving that, you take a rash, rushed, unnatural, jerk knee reaction that you later have to face consequences for. For example, some decisions you make in love become a mind game, resulting in hit or miss decisions that you call experiences but are, in reality, disappointments in front of your mind.

In this situation, is it the mind or the body? There should’ve been heart, but now there is only the brain. This brain is riddled by negative experiences that demand lust and harbor feelings of “the world is not fair.” This slogan has been put in front of our eyes by our dear parents, friends, friends’ parents, and society.

On the first hand, these experiences don’t hurt as much, but they are so vile and wrong, as our friends say. We take on their negativity. For instance, I recall my true nature from childhood. If somebody stole my things and money, I wasn’t affected much. My heart used to say it is okay because they may need that. 

---

> #### **Children are very forgiving.**

![unsplash](/images/essays-assets/unsplash%20(44).jpeg)

Like Passenger’s song “All Little Lights,” we were born with many little lights in our hearts. As we face life, we begin to dim those lights. We were made to believe that money is a bigger thing, and materialistic things, to a child’s brain, were unknown.

Our parents, relatives, and society told us to believe, along with thousands of others, that we are competing among ourselves. Bigger cars meant richer people, and we must aspire to that. We have forgotten ourselves — our true nature, heart, mind, and happiness, which we didn’t lose becoming adults but was taken and consumed by human ego.

We didn’t know about any religion, caste, color, or where anyone is from. Instead, it was certified by our society, validating our parents to form certain negative perceptions. These perceptions were not even true, but our parents trusted society, which they learned from their parents who also trusted society. Just as we trusted our parents, friends, and society, wanting validation due to fears given by our dear ones, we adopted negative perceptions. This unwanted sadness now feels like a betrayal. They warned us against things they didn’t see or know. Their individualism was killed; they killed ours.

> #### As we come in our 20s, we became ‘judgment gurus.’ We got glasses of these perceptions. It turns out you are not racist at all, but as you see certain races, colors, or sexes, you take a step back, evaluate, and don’t trust at all. Don’t worry; they are in the same boat!

As a kid, I never knew Buddhism is a religion in which they don’t eat onions. I didn’t know some poor kids get free education. As soon as I turned to tell that to my parents, my friends, and some people on the street, I got, “These poor bastards, we are actually poor.” Upon asking, they gave me various reasons, not their thoughts but the result of negative thoughts reinforced by society to make norms. I realized the norm breakers are on the rise; however, there are a thousand times more army of norm creators.

In simple terms, usually in small villages where kids stay kids, kids until they want to, where it is not told, “You are 13; you must do certain things,” and peer pressure is not compulsory. We mess ourselves with proactive art that should have been learned — the art of thinking unnaturally.

While this is a process, fear always roots from uncertainty. With the art of doing things uncertainty, the art of doing things you think you’re not capable of, your mind takes shortcuts without preparing to be capable enough. With uncertainty, you play; you may win. But when you lose, fear is born.

> #### **A common trend in society is raised that either it is a brainer or no brainer.**

> #### **Each child is grown differently by god, and some children are capable of doing different things.**

---

But we are pushed to learn uncertainty but never the ways to approach uncertainty. It turns into a permanent fear. A natural mind, I remember from my childhood, didn’t need to give a second thought before doing anything. Either I could do it or I couldn’t, realizing my true potential.

## My Own Story My Own Inspiration 🦋

This is why I love children because I love myself as a kid. I always try to act like a joyful version of me. Until I wasn’t provided people’s uncertainty for me to validate in a group of people that parents were happy to go home and discuss, I was the best version of myself — breathing, thinking, running, philosophizing, and sharing the amazing personality. I was fearless and brave. I love myself as a kid.

I wish I was always trying to reconnect with my true nature, trying to be more patient, more open, more honest, ethical, and happy where I didn’t need to hide that I adopted negative influences of others. It started from frustration, which was not even mine.

But as a citizen of a certain religion and ethnicity, I was born with, I need to take care of being a part of it, which I never joined. Must I have played with friend R instead of friend K in play class period? That was my worry. Except I wasn’t given an ultimatum about their families and me taking a different vision. They were put on a pedestal, where they were my best friends in high school. These frustrations I carried on in adult life. My thought process built on those edges, and now it is automated.

I carried on, in adult life, having taken wrong habits, being unnatural, dishonest, anxious, depressed, an untrustworthy human being who has caused people and myself a lot of pain. This is purely out of unnatural behavior that this kid was altered and was gifted by my dear and loved ones. Not intentionally, as they have been trapped too, that life and the world are unfair.

My perception is if it’s unfair, it doesn’t mean you have to be unfair too. With unfairness comes guilt to yourself, where you always could have been a better human being and not caused the pain. The world would have been a better place for you and me.

With these thoughts, I know people are in the same boat, taking unnatural behavior, living in uncertainty, which they call life but I call a life support. The goal of life is to live it, enjoy it, and let everyone around enjoy. Be a giver of pleasant experiences, eliminating all thoughts of agony, lust, jealousy, and negative things, which were not me when I was 6–7 years old. Why did I change? I don’t know, but I was most happy as life passed by.

> #### At the end, I say to y’all who read this, if you find or found me childish, yes, I am — the child — the pure, natural, jolly, and charming.
