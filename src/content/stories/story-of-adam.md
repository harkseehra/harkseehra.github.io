#### He’s the one, He’s the one, He’s the one

#### ਉਹ ਇੱਕ ਹੈ, ਉਹ ਇੱਕ ਹੈ, ਉਹ ਇੱਕ ਹੈ

#### او ییک است، او ییک است، او ییک است

## As always inspired by ਮੁਰਸ਼ਿਦ ਰੂਮੀ - مولانا رومی - Maulana Rumi

Really old story, but I wanted to tell you the original version I received from God.

When a human was born, or thought to be—planned to be—desired by some people living down on Earth, they requested, they thought—they dreamed about having a child.

God was merciful. He saw that the man and his wife were faithful devotees, and He gave them hope. God said maybe your dreams weren't possible; maybe you had your chance, but the Devil bought it cheaply.

Where you should have been doing hard work and promoting the good mannerisms and culture of our first ancestor—first of our own—first human Adam, originally created by God. But you sold to the Devil by drinking his wine cheaply, or maybe the wine was expensive, but it degraded your value.

God was creating Adam. It likely took Him six days to create our world. As we come into this world, we don’t have vision, and we cannot hear. Out of the mother's womb, the child comes and cries; he or she doesn’t know who the protector is, but the soul knows that there is God.

Similarly, the mother who has given us birth, the child automatically knows this is Mama, and I am safe.

---


Back to the story, Adam was created, and God created him in His own image. God said " Oh my dear Adam—for this is you—and this is me, we are pretty much the same. These are angels who are your protectors. "

Adam was born as a child, as we are. That is why we are children of God. Angels protected him until the age of the lucky number six. He was carefree; he couldn’t speak much, but the laughter of his joy gave God so much happiness.

God created Adam because He loved him. Else why would God create him? As Adam grew up, angels said it is time he should know us.

God said, I have created him in my own image, so he would remember me when he looks at himself in the mirror. I am just making sure he doesn’t worship idols; that is why there is a free will of Adam I should offer him—after all, the child is destined to grow into an adult.

So God said, "Oh Adam, my son, I give you this tongue, teeth, and voice. Whatever you say the name of things would be, it shall be called." 

But our little Adam was still learning. He was about seven when he learned the first alphabet should be `الیف` - Alef. So our little Adam could use the muscles of his mouth to pronounce this first letter, of which languages we have AA, ਓ,  Alef had that magical secret that it could convert to any vowel sounds—pretty basic. 

Then God decided, "Let’s add some tongue action—try the pronunciation of this other magical alphabet, `لام` - Lam - L sound." So when our little Adam tried that, he discovered there is tongue - `زبان` - ਜ਼ੁਬਾਨ.

Until then, our little Adam tried these exercises and felt he was struggling. He looked at God with his teary eyes, having these two magical big letters but finding no relief. 

God said, "Don’t worry, there is another set of letters which are relief letters. One is to keep close to your heart when you remember me, and one is a parable for your children. You know I created you because I loved you, so you are a creature of love - `عشق` - ਇਸ਼ਕ."

Among these two relief letters were `ح` and `ه`, two H sounds. God said, "One is private and one is to let everyone know that you are my child." 

Fast forward, God introduced Adam to the Angels. Adam somehow learned the whole alphabet - letters - sounds and was able to say names—it was just new freedom. He called the angels whatever he felt like, and the Angels accepted.

God said to the angels, "You are of heaven. This is my new creation whom I love most. He is my reflection, so bow down to him."

Everyone bowed to Adam except for the low-bodied `ابلیس` - Eblis, full of ebbs and flows. He was full of pride and said, "Why would I bow down, God, to this little human? I am of fire, he is of clay—what superiority does he have over me?"

God got angry and cursed Satan, and was about to cast him out of paradise. "Oh devil, weren’t you an angel? How did you turn into this? Why have your teeth become sharper as if you want to eat some dead? Why has your face become sour—you are cursed—yet you feel superior. What is this stupidity?"

Satan somehow convinced God and said, "Oh God, your creature has faults. Out of love, you have made a huge mistake. [Often, in love, we make mistakes.]"

It was the Devil's defense; he smirked with his shark teeth.

God sighed and said, "Okay. If you hurt my child with your power, you will face repercussions."

The Devil schemed, and he said, "I will create an illusion. Let's begin with TIME."

So the Devil started keeping an evil eye. Adam was philosophising , playing, and being happy in paradise until the age of 13.

---

---

> ### Creation of Eve **هوا** - First Woman

But over all these years, Adam got bored. He asked God, "Oh God, this is the same routine, just me, you, and these angels. Create something new—amazing."

It was an unusual request, as we share the same flaw: even if we are comfortable, we want more. But God said, "Okay, I will create a phantasy for you."

An image of you, but it is a phantasy of how you would like it. So Adam imagined his wife, Eve, for the first time; he imagined how Eve would be.

Obviously, Adam was purified—his thoughts were pure. You must thank God, oh dear reader, that it was Adam—not you—or the ideal of Eve might have been something unconventional and not very pure.

So Adam was put into a deep sleep by God. He took one of the ribs from his left side at the front, and magically Eve was by his side when he woke up.

So Eve was as Adam imagined, as throughout the whole story—God is merciful, listening, and complying, whether it is Adam or the Devil. As God believes in freedom—`آزادی`—ਅਜ਼ਾਦੀ.

So Adam and Eve took their time to know each other and did some `گُفْتُگُو`—ਗੁਫ਼ਤਗੂ—private talk.

Adam believed in love besides God then. God was the father, Eve was the wife, but the love here is the same. In the same way he loved God, he loved Eve; obviously, he was a purified spirit. 

But the scheming devil—he saw it; he was planning his moves to hurt them. He saw whilst Adam was straight in his spine, his phantasy was constantly changing.

As Eve was—her face, her features were different every day as Adam desired. She was the first woman, and she had qualities to personify beauty a million times over. Adam and Eve grew up and were enjoying their life. Then, at the age of 17, God visited them.

---

> ### Devil’s Plan

God said -

"Oh my beautiful children, `ربی` `نور` - ਰੱਬੀ ਨੂਰ - the glow on your faces are making the sun brighter every day, and the moon of your souls illuminates paradise at night. I have other important affairs. Besides, you have grown up and are free to make your own decisions.”

"Oh Adam and Phantasy (Eve)—the whole of paradise is yours to keep; you reign over here. But I must tell you that there is a forbidden tree of knowledge whose fruit you should avoid.

 This is something for you when you grow up in your 30s, but the tree is there. Don’t eat its fruit. Frankly, this is the fruit of knowledge and judgement; with knowledge comes judgement. It is only better for you to be as you were created, my children."

The devil was standing afar like a thief and hearing it, so, as is his nature as a snake, he was sneaky. 

One day, whilst Adam was resting after meditation and leisure, and Eve was sitting down next to that tree, the Devil took the form of a serpent. He went next to the Phantasy and sneaked his way by whispers. 

*{In whispers, we don’t know who is our friend or enemy.}*

Devil said, “Oh phantasy, oh houri - `حُورٌ` - ਹੂਰ of so many beauties, you are not real—but you are the phantasy of Adam. Adam makes you as he likes you, he commands. Don’t you want freedom?”

Eve was resisting and asked, “Oh, Mister, who are you and what is your business? Declare it, or I will wake Adam immediately.”

The Devil wasted no time and set his trap of greed. “Do you know why God has made that fruit forbidden? It is because that is how we are born. We are angels; we became that by eating that fruit, for God knows, since eternity.”

“The more knowledge you have, the more powerful you are,” were the Devil’s words. Somehow, Eve took the fruit from the Devil and ate it; then she convinced Adam to do the same since she was his phantasy, and Adam thought she was real.

{That is why this love is tricky; it is fantastic and fantasy at the same time.}

As they both ate that fruit—it was probably an apple—and out of greed, both Adam and Eve ate a lot, somehow without chewing properly. As a reminder of greed and a wrong decision, the apple got stuck in Adam’s throat.

The digestion took over in some time, and when it happened, their upper eyes were open—the eyes of free will. But now they had their own eyes—they forgot God and at first realised it was freedom. 

Until the moment they saw each other fully, they were naked, and they started running in all directions to hide themselves.

Now there was guilt as Adam saw Eve—he was ashamed to see his wife’s nakedness. His wife Phantasy got wrinkles, and her beauty faded; she was, out of guilt, trying to hide herself. 

But ultimately, it was God’s creation that Eve would be as Adam imagined. As knowledge came, the God’s consciousness got corrupted. Anyhow, God was free of His affairs and decided to visit Adam and Eve.

God found that they had been unfaithful to their promises and covenants and had eaten from that tree.

 The Devil came in pride to God and smirked, “See, God, didn’t I say they are of clay? You know fire is always above light; light is the creation of fire.”

God sighed, “Oh devil, you think you are the one playing, but you must remember this: ***you didn’t throw when you threw.***”

“You may think it is your schemes, but fantasy had to become reality.”

God called Adam and Eve, who were in disbelief and saw their faces were long and cold. 

God, still being merciful, said, “Since for you, knowledge has taken over my love, and since for you, idols have taken over my flexibility, it was free will; and instead of freely loving me, you broke my covenant.”

Upon hearing these words, Adam and Eve cried for the first time. They repented and said sorry, but as always, God had some other plans which suited better for their behaviour.

God said, “Oh, you are of clay; go back to clay. Earlier, you were only two; there will be thousands and millions like you, whom you will procreate.”

“Since you have trusted this devil, this devil will also go down with you, but as merciful as the one is, and as fair as the one is, the devil will always be beneath you - always scheming and playing.”

“Also, for procreation, now your bodies have two different types - as there are keys and locks. Your children will break down multiple times playing this key and lock game.”

“Always trying to find betterment, your children will try hundreds of keys for their happiness, but their happiness I will put inside them, hidden deep and buried in their hearts. So when they keep digging, they will find it as you will find food while digging the ground.”

Adam and Eve, they came to Earth. They repented, they cried, they worked hard - they cleansed themselves.

---

> ### Ultimatum

Even today, God creates millions of Adams and Eves in infinite universes—each child has a beginning and an end. Some people understand this and choose God over knowledge and judgement. 

Others, having fallen into the same traps as the devil, choose knowledge and sell their `ایمان` - ਇਮਾਨ - faith cheaply for some gold and silver, because knowledge teaches how human beings sell themselves for gold and silver.

But while looking at that gold and silver, people forget God and become unbelievers. Some people, however, realise this and place God above knowledge.

The light is of God, for it was God who was the one who created everything, and at first, He created light. With greed, the fire was born of night as the cursed devil.

End of story
